## Panelists

### Rebecca Vessal
**Senior Gameplay Programmer @ Double Fine Productions**  
*RIT Game Design & Development, Class of 2014*

Rebecca is a game programmer with over a decade of industry experience. She has spent the past seven years at Double Fine Productions, specializing in gameplay and game AI programming. Before joining Double Fine, she worked on a variety of mobile games and applications, building a strong foundation in both technical and creative aspects of game development. Throughout her career, Rebecca has combined her programming expertise with a deep passion for interactive design, contributing to projects that bring humor, personality, and heart to the gaming experience.

---

### Eric Decker
**Senior Engineering Manager, Websites @ Squarespace**  
*RIT New Media Design & Imaging (BFA)*

Eric leads multiple product and platform teams within Squarespace's Websites organization. His teams focus on enhancing the core editing experience of the website builder, delivering major projects such as Fluid Engine, which redefined the platform's layout and design capabilities. They have also pioneered the integration of generative AI-powered features into the CMS and developed advanced animation and interaction tools.

Before Squarespace, Eric spent over 11 years at Firstborn, a renowned New York digital agency, where he rose from Flash Developer to Vice President of Technology. There, he led engineering teams on award-winning digital projects for clients including Pepsi, Supercell, Riot Games, Beats by Dre, and Sony PlayStation. Eric has spoken at industry conferences such as FITC Toronto and OFFF Paris.

---

### Rohit Crasta
**Lead Game Designer @ The New York Times**  
*RIT Game Design & Development, Class of 2013*

Rohit "Ro" Crasta is a veteran game designer and developer with over a decade of experience in casual and mobile games. He began his career in community and event organizing for game developers before moving into hands-on design roles, contributing to titles like Marvel Puzzle Quest and other Facebook and mobile games.

He went on to become Lead Puzzle Designer at Funkatron, where he helped develop Cascade for Big Fish Games and built a thriving internship program that drew many talented students from RIT. Over the years, Ro expanded his expertise across mobile, casino, and iGaming projects, serving as Technical Director at GameCo and later as Lead Designer at PeopleFun, where he led design for the hit word game Wordscapes.

Ro currently works on an upcoming unannounced project at The New York Times. He specializes in puzzle and word game design and enjoys mentoring aspiring developers—often sharing lessons learned from both his successes and missteps along the way.

---

### Sela Davis
**Co-founder @ Studio Lakes BV (Amsterdam, Netherlands)**  
*Former: Xbox, WG Cells (Wargaming), Vreal, Wizards of the Coast*

Sela Davis is 1/2 of Studio Lakes BV in Amsterdam, Netherlands. She has worked in publishing, mobile game development, and platform services throughout her career at Xbox, WG Cells (Wargaming), Vreal, and Wizards of the Coast. She is best known for her work on achievements for the Xbox 360 and the Xbox One.

While she specializes in building, deploying, and debugging game technologies, she is also deeply passionate about helping people of underrepresented genders enter the game industry, and spent several years helping to manage the IGDA Foundation Velocity scholarship. Outside of work, she is a metal artist, former keytarist for The Last Book Club, solo percussive acoustic guitarist, and maker of shiny things.

## Why Attend?

This panel brings together IGM alumni who have built careers across gaming, tech, and media. Whether you're interested in gameplay programming, puzzle design, engineering management, or entrepreneurship, these panelists offer insights from diverse paths through the industry.

They'll be sharing lessons learned so you don't have to make the same mistakes—candid advice from people who were once sitting exactly where you are now.
