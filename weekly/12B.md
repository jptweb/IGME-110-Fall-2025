## III. Optional Figma Polish: Variables & Toggle Buttons

### The Big Question: How Polished Should Your Prototype Be?

**Remember:** You're building a **prototype**, not a working app!

**Think back to your lo-fi testing:**
- Users were "thinking aloud" while you showed screens
- You could swap screens manually
- Form fields didn't need to actually work
- That was OK - it's a prototype!

**The same applies to hi-fi:**
- Stakeholders viewing your prototype understand it's not a real app
- You draw the line somewhere - perfect is the enemy of done
- A prototype that looks very close to the real thing can really sell the concept
- BUT if it takes weeks/months to build, it defeats the purpose

**Prototypes are meant to be built BEFORE the app, not become the app.**

### Figma Variables & Toggle Buttons (Optional Advanced Techniques)

If you want to add extra polish and have time, here are some techniques:

**Variables in Figma:**
- Create reusable values (colors, spacing, etc.)
- Change them once, update everywhere
- Useful for consistent design systems
- [Intro to Variables Video By Figma Team](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1ONxxlJnvdM) (a bit overkill for what we need but I couldnt find much briefer one)

**Toggle Buttons (Interactive Components):**

- Advanced example with light/dark mode: [Advanced Toggle (12:23)](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=DayAH0OrRuI)
  - Note: Figma UI has changed slightly, so parts of this video may not match exactly
- **[FIGMA Demo of Toggle and light/dark mode](https://www.figma.com/design/0ozlAyDq0Vu0oeu4yuEZGC/Light-Mode-And-Variable-Demo?node-id=0-1&t=aWdfQ7uXrEVXnAhQ-1)** Here is a simple demo I made of light/dark mode and a toggle button.
